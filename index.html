I am a researcher at the Language Technology Lab at DFKI. My research work is currently funded by the META-NET project, focusing on »optimising the division of labour in hybrid machine translation«.

As part of my research work, I am co-organiser of the Second Workshop on Applying Machine Learning Techniques to Optimise the Division of Labour in Hybrid MT (ML4HMT-12) co-joined with COLING 2012 in Mumbai, India.

I have studied computer science at Saarland University and submitted my diploma thesis in July 2007. My PhD project is titled «A Machine Learning Framework for System Combination Using Joint, Binarised Feature Vectors».

See my publication/citation overview on Google Scholar. My Curriculum Vitae is available on request.

Reseach interests

My research interests include but are not limited to:

Machine Translation
Combining both statistical and rule-based MT into working, hybrid systems.
MT Evaluation
Extending MT evaluation efforts moving from automated scores to human annotation.
Knowledge Distribution
Creating a scalable, distributed network providing access to language resources.
Information Extraction
Retrieving information about companies or products from unstructured sources.
